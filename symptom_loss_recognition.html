Severe Symptoms

Loss of Recognition (Agnosia)

One of the most emotionally challenging stages for family members is when their loved one can no longer recognize faces, names, or even their own reflection.

Understanding the Impact

What is Agnosia?

Agnosia is the inability to process sensory information. In Alzheimer's, this most commonly manifests as Prosopagnosia‚Äîthe inability to recognize faces, including those of lifelong partners and children.

Why it Happens

As the disease progresses into the moderate and severe stages, the damage spreads to the temporal and occipital lobes, which are responsible for storing and retrieving facial and object templates.

The Emotional Weight

For caregivers, being looked at like a stranger by a parent or spouse is often described as "the grieving before the death." It is a profound test of emotional endurance.

How to Respond

Don't Test Memory "Do you know who I am?" or "Remember my name?" often causes anxiety. Instead, introduce yourself gently: "Hi Mom, it's your daughter, Sarah."
Focus on Emotions Even if they don't know *who* you are, they often recognize the *feeling* of safety and love your presence provides. Connect through touch, tone, and music.
Use Old Photos Sometimes black and white photos or images from their youth can trigger recognition better than current images, as those memories are deeper.
